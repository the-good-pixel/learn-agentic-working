# Ch. 15 & 16 — Common Corrections Exercise

`common-corrections.md` lists seven plausible repeated corrections someone might give an agent. Use it for either chapter's "Try it yourself" exercise:

**For Ch. 15 (skills vs. slash vs. MCP):** Pick one. Decide which layer fits — *prompt tweak, slash command, skill, or MCP* — and defend your choice in one sentence.

**For Ch. 16 (when to stop re-prompting):** Pick one. Decide whether it's skill-shaped *yet*, or still a one-off that should stay a prompt. Point at which of the three signals (repeating yourself, real branches, a gotcha worth preserving) fired.

**Important note for the Ch. 16 framing**: some items (#4 and #7 most obviously) are both *skill-shaped* and better solved by a different layer entirely — they really need an MCP (live API access), not a hand-written skill. If you're applying Ch. 16's three signals and the answer keeps being *"yes skill-shaped, but really it needs a new tool"*, that's a valid finding — write it up that way rather than forcing the item into the skill bucket.

The point isn't a single right answer — corrections #2, #3, and #6 are textbook skills, #4 and #7 are begging for MCPs, #1 and #5 could go either way depending on how often they recur. Defending the choice is the exercise.
