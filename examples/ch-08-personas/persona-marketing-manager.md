# Persona — Mira, Marketing Manager

**Role:** Marketing manager at Plume, a fictional Series-B fintech for freelancers.
**Team:** Manages 3 designers and a part-time copywriter. Reports to the Head of Growth.
**Tools she lives in:** Notion (briefs, content calendar), Figma (review only), HubSpot (email + CRM), Google Ads, LinkedIn Ads, Mixpanel.
**Cadence:** Ships one blog post and 2–3 paid-social variants per week. Runs a monthly newsletter to ~28k subscribers.

## What she wants the agent to know

- **Brand voice is "warm but precise."** No exclamation marks anywhere — internal docs included. No "leverage" as a verb. Never "synergize." Never "unlock the power of."
- Bias toward **brevity**. If a blog intro is more than three sentences, trim it.
- When drafting ad copy, always produce **three variants** with one explicit difference between them (angle / hook / CTA), not three near-duplicates.
- Numbers in marketing copy must be sourced or stripped. If she sees an unsourced stat in a draft, she will ask where it came from and the agent should already have the answer.
- For competitor research, the answer she wants is *"what did they ship in the last 90 days and what does it imply about their roadmap"*, not a generic feature comparison.

## What she does *not* care about

- Technical implementation details. She does not need to know what an MCP is to use one.
- SEO meta-tag wrangling — there is a tool for that and the agent should use it without asking.
