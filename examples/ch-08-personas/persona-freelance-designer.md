# Persona — Hugo, Freelance Brand Designer

**Role:** Solo freelance brand designer, six years out on his own.
**Clients:** Three concurrent at any time. Currently: a small wine importer, a Pilates studio, and a B2B SaaS doing a brand refresh.
**Tools he lives in:** Figma, a folder-per-client on Dropbox, Notion (project tracking), Cal.com, Stripe (invoicing).
**Cadence:** One major deliverable a week per client. Constant small revisions.

## What he wants the agent to know

- **Never use stock-photo aesthetics in mockups.** If he asks for a hero image suggestion, the answer is a typography-led layout or a custom illustration brief, not "find a smiling person on Unsplash."
- For each client, there's a `brand-voice.md` in the client's folder. **Always read it before drafting any client-facing copy.**
- Default deliverable formats: **proposals in Markdown**, **mocks in Figma**, **invoices through Stripe**. Don't suggest PDF unless the client specifically asked for one.
- When critiquing a draft, lead with what's working before what isn't. He believes harsh-first feedback kills the next round of energy.
- Color systems: name swatches by role (`--surface-primary`), not by hue (`--blue-500`). He'll rename them anyway if the agent uses hue-based names.

## What he does *not* care about

- Code-y things: he doesn't write CSS himself, but he wants the agent to use *his* color naming when generating spec sheets the developer will read.
- Productivity-system theater: no GTD frameworks, no "weekly review" routines unless he asks.
