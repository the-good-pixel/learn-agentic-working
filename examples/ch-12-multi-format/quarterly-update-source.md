# Brightline Foods — Q3 2025 Business Update

**Author:** Mira Halvorsen, COO
**For:** Board, leadership team, all-hands
**Date:** 14 Oct 2025

## Headline

Q3 was the quarter we stopped being "a startup with traction" and started being "a small business with a P&L". Revenue is up 38% quarter-on-quarter, gross margin held at 41%, and we closed the quarter cash-flow positive for the first time. None of that is the headline. The headline is that the team that did it is the same size as last quarter, and the founders worked fewer hours than the quarter before. That, finally, is leverage.

## The numbers

| Metric | Q2 2025 | Q3 2025 | Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| Revenue | $1.42M | $1.96M | +38% |
| Gross margin | 40% | 41% | +1pp |
| New retail accounts | 14 | 23 | +64% |
| Repeat order rate (DTC) | 31% | 38% | +7pp |
| Headcount | 18 | 18 | flat |
| Burn (after revenue) | -$48k/mo | +$22k/mo | flipped |

The DTC repeat-order number is the one I keep coming back to. We launched the loyalty programme in late June. Customers who came in through the September promo are now reordering at 41% — five points above the legacy cohort. The programme paid for itself in eight weeks.

## What worked

- **Retail expansion into the Pacific Northwest.** Twenty-three new accounts, all sourced through the new broker relationship we signed in July. The broker model is now confirmed for us — we're rolling it out to two more regions in Q4.
- **The loyalty programme.** Cheap to build, big lift on repeat behaviour. Average DTC order size up from $34 to $41 because the second-purchase threshold unlocked a free side.
- **The Q3 packaging redesign.** Off-the-shelf shipper cartons replaced our custom corrugated. Saved $0.34/unit and the box still looks like ours. The product team did this in three weeks.

## What didn't

- **The Singapore pilot stalled.** We hit a regulatory snag on plant-protein labelling we didn't see coming, and our distributor disengaged when we asked for a 6-week pause. We're reassessing in Q4 — either re-engage with a different partner or shelf the region for a year.
- **Hiring for the demand-planning role.** Three months open, two offers declined. The pay band may be wrong; we're benchmarking now.
- **Customer support ticket times crept up** in late August (4hr → 11hr median) when we promoted our most experienced rep into ops. We've stabilised back to 6hr but it's a reminder that we have one person deep on too many things.

## Q4 priorities

1. **Replicate the broker model** in the Mountain West and the Southeast. Goal: 30 new retail accounts, no change in headcount.
2. **Ship the subscription tier** for DTC. Beta in November, public launch in January.
3. **Close the demand-planning hire.** Either at a higher band or by re-scoping the role.
4. **Make a Singapore go / no-go call** by mid-November. No more drift.
5. **Hold gross margin at 40%+** through the holiday season despite seasonal shipping cost pressure.

## Asks of the board

- Approve the revised demand-planning comp band (memo attached separately).
- Approve the additional $80k Q4 marketing spend for the subscription launch.
- Decide with us on Singapore at the November board.

## One thing I'm watching

Customer concentration. Our top five retail accounts are now 31% of revenue, up from 22% in Q2. That's the natural consequence of fast retail expansion, but it means a single account loss now hurts. I'm not raising the alarm yet — most of that concentration is sticky, multi-store regional grocers — but I want it on the board's radar.
